MP3 Writeup

Overview and Usage

For MP3, I decided to recreate Dong Nguyen's "Flappy Bird" game.

Development Process

I broke this project up into several steps to complete it:

  1. First, I followed this Platform Tutorial that teaches you how to build an endless runner.

  2. Once I got the tutorial working, I began making changes to it to make it gradually more similar to a Flappy Bird game. For example, I made the ground all green and the obstacles into long rectangles instead of squares randomly scattered across the screen. I made two obstacle sprites, an upper and a lower one, then created a gap in between them for the player to jump through.


    In progress screenshot of MP3 Game

  3. Then, I randomized where the gaps will be and loaded in images for the bird, background, and ground. I also changed the scoring so that points will only increase by one every time the player jumps through a pair of pipes.


Issue Deep-Dive

An issue I ran into during MP2 was getting Sketch 6 to work as intended. I wanted to draw a sine wave in which its wavelength and amplitude depend on the frequency and amplitude of the sound waves produced by the user dragging their mouse across the screen, respectively. The main difficulty of this was scaling it to fit on the display screen appropriately.

To solve this issue, I had to scale the angle and y accordingly. I achieved this by a simple process of trial and error. Eventually I settled on scaling the angle by a factor of 0.01, and the y by a factor of 200, and this did the trick.

Ideas and Future

Here are some ideas and features I might like to add to my project in the future:

  • Adding in special arcade fonts for the score keeper and end of game message.

  • Rather than tapping anywhere to restart, having buttons for "Play Again" or "Quit."

  • Loading in music and adding a sound for every pair of pipes the player jumps through, and a sound for when the player dies.

  • Including a score tracker that keeps track of the player's highest score, then after a game is over displaying both the score from that game as well as the high score. Also, displaying the high score at the beginning of the screen.
